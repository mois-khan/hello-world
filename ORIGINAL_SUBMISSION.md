## Idea
Problem Statement

India has over 6.2 crore undigitised land records, and land disputes remain one of the country's largest sources of civil litigation, often taking years to resolve. Fragmented records, manual verification, document forgery, and the absence of a single trusted source of ownership create delays, fraud, and uncertainty for citizens, businesses, and government agencies.

Solution

We are building a Blockchain-based Land Registry on a permissioned blockchain network that serves as a tamper-evident, shared source of truth for land ownership. Every property transfer is digitally signed by the buyer, seller, and authorized registrar before being permanently recorded on the blockchain. Smart contracts validate transactions, ensuring that ownership records cannot be altered without authorization.

Key Features

- Permissioned blockchain for secure government-managed access
- Cryptographically signed ownership transfers
- Smart contract-based transaction validation
- Immutable and auditable ownership history
- Instant public verification of property ownership
- Transparent and fraud-resistant record management

Impact

Our solution reduces document fraud, increases transparency, simplifies ownership verification, and minimizes administrative delays. By creating a trusted digital land registry, it has the potential to significantly reduce land ownership disputes and strengthen public trust in property transactions. Inspired by India's Digital India mission and successful international initiatives in countries such as the UAE and Sweden, our platform demonstrates how blockchain can modernize land governance.
